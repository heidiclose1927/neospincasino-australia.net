2. Recognizing Problem Gambling

Gambling should be a form of entertainment, not a source of financial or emotional distress. You may be experiencing problem gambling if you:

  • Spend more money or time gambling than you can afford.
  • Neglect work, family, or social obligations due due to gambling.
  • Try to chase your losses or gamble to recover money.
  • Lie about your gambling habits.
  • Feel anxious, guilty, or depressed because of gambling.
  • Borrow money or sell possessions to gamble.
  • Have difficulty stopping or controlling your gambling.

If you are concerned about your gambling habits or those of a loved one, we urge you to seek help.
